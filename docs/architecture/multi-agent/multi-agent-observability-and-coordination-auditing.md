# Multi‑Agent Observability and Coordination Auditing

## Purpose

Multi‑Agent Observability and Coordination Auditing provide visibility into multi‑agent communication, coordination, task allocation, and decision‑making. This system ensures transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement of multi‑agent behavior.

---

## Observability Components

- **Coordination Logs** — record inter‑agent communication and task delegation.
- **Decision Traces** — capture reasoning and action selection across agents.
- **Conflict Logs** — document coordination conflicts and resolutions.
- **Safety & Ethics Logs** — track filtered messages and blocked actions.
- **Performance Metrics** — coordination latency, task distribution efficiency.

---

## Auditing Objectives

- Detect unsafe or inconsistent multi‑agent behavior.
- Ensure fairness and transparency across agents.
- Provide traceability for distributed decisions.
- Support governance and compliance reviews.
- Identify coordination bottlenecks and inefficiencies.

---

## Auditing Pipeline

1. Collect multi‑agent events and metrics.
2. Classify events by subsystem and coordination type.
3. Analyze patterns, anomalies, and regressions.
4. Generate structured audit reports.
5. Feed insights into governance, safety, and coordination improvements.
