# Agent Behavior in Simulated Environments

## Purpose

Agent Behavior in Simulated Environments defines how agents perceive, reason, act, and adapt within virtual worlds. It ensures that agent behavior remains coherent, safe, and aligned with cognitive and sensorimotor architectures.

---

## Behavioral Components

- **Perceptual Behavior** — interpreting simulated sensory inputs.
- **Cognitive Behavior** — reasoning about goals, constraints, and environment state.
- **Motor Behavior** — executing actions through simulated actuators.
- **Adaptive Behavior** — adjusting strategies based on feedback.
- **Social Behavior** — interacting with other agents in shared environments.

---

## Behavior Workflow

1. Receive sensory input from simulation.
2. Update internal cognitive and perceptual state.
3. Generate reasoning outputs and action plans.
4. Execute actions through motor control or symbolic commands.
5. Evaluate outcomes and refine behavior.

---

## Behavior Types

- **Goal-Driven Behavior** — pursuing tasks or objectives.
- **Reactive Behavior** — responding to immediate stimuli.
- **Exploratory Behavior** — gathering information or mapping environment.
- **Collaborative Behavior** — coordinating with other agents.
- **Safe Behavior** — avoiding hazards and respecting constraints.

---

## Safety Integration

- Unsafe actions are blocked before execution.
- Behavior must respect environment safety rules.
- Ethical constraints apply to social and collaborative behavior.
- Risk detection triggers fallback or escalation.

---

## Integration with Other Systems

- **Cognitive Architecture v2** — drives reasoning and decision-making.
- **Sensorimotor Architecture v2** — provides perception and motor control.
- **Multi-Agent Coordination Architecture v2** — governs collaborative behavior.
- **Testing & Validation Framework v2** — evaluates behavior in simulation.

---

## Design Principles

- Behavior must be interpretable and auditable.
- Agents must remain consistent across simulation runs.
- Behavior must adapt without violating safety or ethics.

---

## Future Extensions

- Multi-agent emergent behavior modeling.
- Simulation-driven cognitive adaptation.
- Domain-specific behavioral modules.
