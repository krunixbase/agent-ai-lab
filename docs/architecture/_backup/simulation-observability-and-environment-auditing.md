# Simulation Observability and Environment Auditing

## Purpose

Simulation Observability and Environment Auditing provide visibility into simulation behavior, agent interactions, environment dynamics, and safety compliance. This system ensures transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement of simulation-based workflows.

---

## Observability Components

- **State Logs** — environment states, object positions, and dynamics.
- **Action Logs** — agent actions, motor commands, and symbolic operations.
- **Perception Logs** — sensory outputs and perceptual interpretations.
- **Safety Logs** — blocked actions, hazard detections, and risk events.
- **Performance Metrics** — simulation speed, fidelity, and resource usage.

---

## Auditing Objectives

- Detect unsafe or inconsistent simulation behavior.
- Ensure environment rules are applied correctly.
- Validate agent behavior against safety and ethical constraints.
- Provide traceability for simulation-driven decisions.
- Support debugging, evaluation, and research workflows.

---

## Auditing Pipeline

1. Collect simulation events and metrics.
2. Classify events by subsystem and risk level.
3. Analyze patterns, anomalies, and regressions.
4. Generate structured audit reports.
5. Feed insights into environment, safety, and behavior improvements.

---

## Integration with Other Systems

- **Safety Architecture** — validates simulation safety.
- **Governance Architecture v2** — oversees simulation accountability.
- **Testing & Validation Framework v2** — uses logs for evaluation.
- **Deployment Architecture v2** — adapts observability to environment constraints.

---

## Design Principles

- Observability must be comprehensive and structured.
- Auditing must be deterministic and reproducible.
- Logs must respect privacy and ethical constraints.
- Insights must directly inform system evolution.

---

## Future Extensions

- Real-time simulation dashboards.
- Multi-agent simulation auditing.
- Predictive simulation anomaly detection.
