# Agent Embodied & Sensorimotor Architecture v2 Overview

## Purpose

Agent Embodied & Sensorimotor Architecture v2 defines how an agent perceives, interprets, and interacts with physical or simulated environments through sensorimotor channels. It provides the conceptual and operational foundation for embodiment, enabling agents to sense, act, adapt, and coordinate within spatial, temporal, and dynamic contexts.

---

## Core Responsibilities

- Integrate multimodal sensory inputs into coherent perceptual representations.
- Support motor control, action execution, and environment manipulation.
- Maintain spatial awareness, object tracking, and situational context.
- Enable adaptive behavior based on sensorimotor feedback loops.
- Ensure safety, ethics, and governance constraints in embodied actions.
- Provide interfaces for simulation, robotics, virtual environments, and hybrid setups.
- Support coordination between cognitive, perceptual, and motor subsystems.

---

## Embodiment Models

- **Virtual Embodiment** — interaction within simulated or digital environments.
- **Robotic Embodiment** — physical actuation through robotic hardware.
- **Hybrid Embodiment** — combination of virtual and physical sensing/acting.
- **Abstract Embodiment** — symbolic or high-level action spaces without direct physics.

---

## Integration with Other Systems

- **Cognitive Architecture v2** — provides goals, reasoning, and action selection.
- **Planning & Reasoning Architecture** — decomposes tasks into sensorimotor actions.
- **Memory Architecture v2** — stores perceptual maps and sensorimotor traces.
- **Safety & Ethics Architectures** — constrain physical actions and interactions.
- **Deployment Architecture v2** — adapts embodiment to environment capabilities.

---

## Design Principles

- Embodiment must be modular and environment‑agnostic.
- Sensorimotor loops must be deterministic and interpretable.
- Safety must override all motor actions.
- Perception and action must remain tightly coupled.
- Embodiment must scale from simple simulations to complex robotics.

---

## Future Extensions

- Multi‑agent embodied coordination.
- Adaptive sensorimotor learning.
- Real‑time embodied cognition.
