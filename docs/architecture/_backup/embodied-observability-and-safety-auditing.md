# Embodied Observability and Safety Auditing

## Purpose

Embodied Observability and Safety Auditing provide visibility into sensorimotor behavior, physical interactions, and embodied decision‑making. This system ensures transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement of embodied performance and safety.

---

## Observability Components

- **Sensor Logs** — raw and processed sensory data.
- **Motor Logs** — executed actions, trajectories, and actuator signals.
- **Environment Logs** — spatial maps, object states, and dynamic changes.
- **Safety Logs** — blocked actions, risk detections, and emergency stops.
- **Performance Metrics** — accuracy, latency, stability, and energy usage.

---

## Auditing Objectives

- Detect unsafe or inconsistent embodied behavior.
- Ensure compliance with safety, ethics, and governance policies.
- Provide traceability for physical interactions and decisions.
- Support debugging, simulation, and robotics evaluation.
- Enable data‑driven improvements to sensorimotor systems.

---

## Auditing Pipeline

1. Collect sensorimotor events and metrics.
2. Classify events by subsystem and risk level.
3. Analyze patterns, anomalies, and regressions.
4. Generate structured audit reports.
5. Feed insights into safety, control, and perception improvements.

---

## Integration with Other Systems

- **Safety Architecture** — validates embodied safety.
- **Runtime Layer** — provides execution context.
- **Deployment Architecture v2** — adapts observability to environment.
- **Governance Architecture v2** — oversees embodied accountability.
- **Testing & Validation Framework v2** — evaluates embodied behavior.

---

## Design Principles

- Observability must be comprehensive and structured.
- Safety auditing must be deterministic and reproducible.
- Logs must respect privacy and ethical constraints.
- Insights must directly inform system evolution.
