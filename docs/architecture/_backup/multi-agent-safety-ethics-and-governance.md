# Multi‑Agent Safety, Ethics, and Governance

## Purpose

Multi‑Agent Safety, Ethics, and Governance define how safety, ethical principles, and governance policies are enforced across multiple agents operating in shared or distributed environments. This system ensures that multi‑agent behavior remains aligned with organizational values and regulatory requirements.

---

## Safety Responsibilities

- Enforce safety constraints across inter‑agent communication.
- Validate delegated tasks for safety and ethical compliance.
- Prevent unsafe emergent behavior from agent interactions.
- Maintain isolation between agents with different safety levels.

---

## Ethical Responsibilities

- Ensure fairness across agents and users.
- Prevent biased or discriminatory multi‑agent behavior.
- Maintain transparency in multi‑agent decisions.
- Protect user autonomy across distributed workflows.

---

## Governance Responsibilities

- Track accountability for multi‑agent decisions.
- Enforce policy compliance across distributed agents.
- Provide escalation pathways for multi‑agent conflicts.
- Support human oversight for high‑risk multi‑agent behavior.

---

## Risk Management

- Detect unsafe coordination patterns.
- Identify conflicting goals or unsafe delegation.
- Trigger escalation when multi‑agent risks exceed thresholds.

---

## Integration with Other Systems

- **Safety Architecture** — enforces cross‑agent safety.
- **Ethics Architecture v2** — ensures fairness and autonomy.
- **Governance Architecture v2** — oversees multi‑agent accountability.
- **Runtime Layer** — applies safety and governance constraints.

---

## Design Principles

- Safety and ethics must scale across agents.
- Governance must remain transparent and auditable.
- Multi‑agent behavior must remain deterministic and controlled.

---

## Future Extensions

- Distributed safety negotiation.
